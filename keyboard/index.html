WildKey
A privacy-aware keyboard toolkit for data collection in-the-wild
With our keyboard you will be able to collect everyday typing data enabling continuous and spontaneous assessments. Try out our Demo available at the Playstore (Android 6.0+).
Data Collection
Implicit Typing Collection
Continually assess users typing behaviours by analysing written text in any and all applications. Your users do not have to do anything other than installing the device. Do not worry if you are reading this and concerned about user privacy! No text or any data that would enable its reconstruction is recorded during implicit collection. All metrics are calculated locally on the device and only metrics with no textual content are sent and stored in the cloud database. Explore the over 15 different metrics WildKey is able to provide on text and touch behaviours.
Explicit Typing Collection
Need more control over your text-entry collection? Want to collect low level touch events and raw text content? With WildKey you are able to create structured text-entry tasks for your users to either transcribe a set of sentences or respond to a set of questions you make! Schedule and send out text-entry tasks for your users to perform on the go. Collect raw text-entered and raw touch point data for your models or questions.
Questionnaires
We know from experience the effort it takes to conduct serious in-the-wild research. To facilitate collecting and cross-reference the data you want from your studies, WildKey enables you to create questionnaires to schedule, and send out to your participants. Through the questionnaire builder, you will be able to deploy your custom questionnaires, digital scales and much more. Your participants will receive a notification and other nudges to fill-out the questionnaire deployed through our WildKey app. We are always adding new questions types and more. Let us know if there is something you need and is missing!
Customizable tasks
If your study requires a more personal touch with very specific tasks WildKey has got you covered. WildKey was developed to be a highly extendable toolkit supporting the creation of custom made-tasks. Currently, you can checkout the feature through the example task the Alternate Finger Tapping test (Lee CY et. al. ).
Privacy-aware
Smartphones are becoming an extension of oneself and data privacy has become of the utmost importance. Text content can be one of the most private types of data users generate and approaches that seek to collect this type of information can expect to be met with resistance by some users and overall suffer from lower levels of adherence and compliance. Aware of the challenges WildKey was devised to not store any textual content outside the specific tasks it asks users to perform. WildKey analyzes all data locally on the device before it synchronizes only those calculations. You and your users get the best of both worlds, privacy and informative metrics.
Private mode
At any point users can select the lock always available when the keyboard is opened to enter the “private mode” which disables all text analytics. No data is processed or stored while the user remains in private mode.
Right to be Forgotten
To facilitate GDPR compliance WildKey enables researchers to quickly download all data from a specific user, and delete it all if necessary.
Study management
WildKey toolkit is bundled with React app to make it easier for you to create, manage and deploy your user studies. The React app has two key features: study management and metrics & analytics. The application is currently deployable with local hosting ensuring you are in control of your WildKey ecosystem. The app allows you to:
  • Personalize your study tasks
    • Text-entry
    • Questionnaires
    • Custom-Made
  • Flexible task schedules
    • Set tasks Individually or for groups of users
  • Notify users
    • Nudge users to complete tasks through keyboard and system level notifications
  • Manage Users
  • Quickly access relevant study metrics through the Study Dashboard

Ongoing Studies
Exploring privacy, adherence and compliance
In this study we explore and evaluate different strategies for text-entry free-living data collection (transcriptions, compositions and implicit) and assess their impact on privacy, adherence and compliance.
Text-entry metrics and Parkinson
We believe there is great potential to have situated assessments of motor skills which may be affected in Parkinson’s disease (PD) patients. In this work, we investigate whether text-entry metrics are promising digital end-points for patients with PD.
Text-entry metrics and fatigue
Together with Open Lab at Newcastle we are exploring how the different metrics and collection methods correlate with fatigue assessment scales.
