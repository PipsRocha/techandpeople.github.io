Overview

The eating patterns of Portuguese children have undergone important changes over time. A national study carried out with children aged 4 (project coorte Geração XXI) found that only 45% of children eat vegetables served on the plate daily, and 65% of them consume cakes and sweets at least once a day. It is widely recognized that the promotion of healthy eating habits should start early in the child's life and focus on the parents' eating practices, that is, on the strategies, actions or behaviors used to influence, intentionally or not, eating behavior (e.g., pressure to eat, reduce children's unhealthy foods accessibility).

SmartFeeding4Kids program

The SmartFeeding4Kids program is a 7-session, self-guided web-based intervention that aims to help parents to improve their young children’s eating habits, by adopting more responsive feeding practices. The intervention was specifically designed to improve children’s consumption of vegetables, fruits, and legumes, and decrease the intake of sugar-sweetened foods and beverages. The program was based on a self-regulation approach aiming at changing parents' feeding practices, habits, and perception of barriers, with individual goal-setting, (self) monitoring, individualized feedback, and modeling of effective strategies as the main methodologies.

About

About the research project

The SmartFeeding4Kids program was developed under the research project “Food Parenting: study of a new web-based parent intervention”, which was national funded by the FCT - Fundação para a Ciência e a Tecnologia, I.P. (PTDC/PSI-GER/30432/2017) with a grant of 209.021,35 euros. The intervention was developed and implemented by a team of researchers in the areas of Psychology, Computer Science and Nutrition and is based on the most recent evidence-based research in this field.

This project aims to develop, pilot test, and study the efficacy of the SmartFeeding4Kids program, a 7- session, self-guided web-based intervention to help parents to improve their young children’s eating habits through responsive feeding practices.

An Randomized Control Trial design was used to study:

  1. the efficacy of the SmartFeeding4Kids program regarding children’s consumption of vegetables, fruits, legumes, and sugar-sweetened foods and beverages, and parental feeding practices, compared with a psychoeducational control condition throughout four assessment moments;
  2. the role of parent’s and child's dimensions (e.g., child’s temperament, parent’s perceived barriers to promote child’s healthy eating habits) as predictors of change and of dropout rates, and;
  3. evolution of children’s eating patterns and parental feeding practices, motivation to change, and feeding habits formation throughout the intervention.

About the SmartFeeding4Kids app

We designed and developed an application in Portuguese, the SmartFeeding4Kids app, to deliver all the intervention components and manage the trial. It can be used on web browsers or as an app on a mobile device and includes seven sessions that cater components from randomization, onboarding, 24 h food recall, delivery of information, personalized feedback to data collection (e.g., study questionnaires), and logging. Aba, an avocado avatar, welcomes and accompanies parents in the application, guiding, informing, and motivating them through the sessions. Charts are presented to illustrate each session’s performance based on food diaries. To further fuel parents’ engagement, badges are given when they complete pre-defined achievements. Notifications and reminders are automatically sent via email to congratulate users for their performance, remind them of pending tasks, and announce new content.

About the recruitment

The ongoing recruitment began in July 2021 and was disseminated in the community through public and private nurseries and kindergartens, parents' associations, and health centers; the social networks Facebook and Instagram; and, press release in the media and school newspapers. Face-to-face/online meetings were held with several educational and health institutions, involving school directors, parents, and/or kindergarten teachers. Schools who agreed to participate also received pamphlets and posters alluding to the program.
