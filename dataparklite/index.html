Metrics
Wear Time
Wear time is defined as consecutive stationary episodes lasting for at least 30 minutes where acceleration magnitude has a standard deviation of more than 13.0 mg (g=9.81m/s2) (van Hees et al. 2011). Non-wear time periods are removed from subsequent analysis. Wear time of less than 75% of a day is a criterion for exclusion of that complete day from further analysis.
Immobility Measures
Immobility measures are captured resorting to the Estimation of Stationary Sleep segments algorithm (Borazio et al. 2014) that looks for long periods of idleness in the accelerometer signal. From this segmentation, several measures will be derived per user per day of analysis:
  • Night Time Immobility Duration (NTID)
  • Night Time Mobility Periods and Duration (NTMP and NTMD)
  • Diurnal Immobility Periods and Duration (DIP and DID)
Additionally, other variables will be derived from the activity signal that account for the transitions and duration of moments of mobility and immobility, within and between days, particularly the following non-parametric indices of circadian rhythmicity (Jones, Hare, and Evershed 2005; Gonçalves et al. 2014):
  • Intradaily variability (IV): looks at fragmentation of rhythm, by assessing the frequency and extent of transitions between rest and activity.
  • Interdaily stability (IS): Indicates invariability of the 24 h rhythm between days. Requires that at least two days are eligible for analysis (see Wear time for exclusion criteria for each day).
  • Relative amplitude (RA): Indicates quantity of activity, calculated from the most active 10 h period and least active 5 h period in an average 24 h pattern.
Activity Measures
Activity measures reflect the amount and robustness of physical activity performed by the subjects. We derive the following measures (O’Brien et al. 2017):
  • Daily Physical Activity (DPA): mean acceleration magnitude, a measure for how much the device was accelerated, in 1 minute. Activity within this period will be the average of the activity during the self-reported day time. The daily period will be the average of all minutes since waking up, until going to bed, including naps;
  • Nightly Physical Activity (NPA): mean acceleration magnitude, a measure for how much the device was accelerated, in 1 minute. Activity within this period will be the average of the activity during the self-reported night time. The nightly period includes wake-up periods;
  • Daily Jerk (DJ): reflects how sudden movements are and is calculated as the mean of the first derivative of acceleration magnitude. Jerk will be averaged per minute for the self-reported daily period.
